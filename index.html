Powerful Women

Amelia Earhart

Amelia Earhart was the first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean. Amelia developed an interest in caring for soldiers wounded in World War I. In 1918 she left junior college to become a nurses aide in Toronto. After the war, Earhart entered the premed program at Columbia University in New York City but left in 1920 after her parents insisted that she live with them in California. She went on her first airplane ride in 1920, an experience that prompted her to take flying lessons. Her disappearance was during a flight around the world in 1937

Harriet Tubman

Harriet Tubman guided many slaves to freedom as the conductor of the Underground Railroad. Harriet Tubman escaped to freedom in the North in 1849. She was nicknamed Moses and was never captured and neither were the people she guided. She is known to this day for the sacrifices she made to bring others freedom.

Rosa Parks

Rosa Parks was a civil rights activist who refused to surrender her seat to a white passenger on a segregated bus in Montgomery, Alabama and her defiance sparked the Montgomery Bus Boycott. After the Montgomery bus boycott, Rosa Parks became an international icon of resistance to racial segregation. She served as secretary in the Montgomery division of NAACP for 14 years from 1943 to 1957 during which she played a key role in mobilizing support for the rape victim Recy Taylor and bringing the incident to national notice.

Maya Angelou

Maya Angelou was an actor, dancer and journalist. Due to childhood sexual abuse and trauma, Angelou became unable to speak for several years. Despite the assassinations of Malcolm X and King, in 1965 and 1968 respectively, in 1969 Angelou published her most famous work, "I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings." This book was hailed for its revolutionary approach and became a part of the movement.
